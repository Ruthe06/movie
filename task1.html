Saba Nayagan

2023. 2h 35m

Saba Nayagan Movie Review: Saba Nayagan is the latest addition to the already long list of
films that revolve around a man and the women he falls in love with at different stages of his life.,
It has to be said that a film like this cannot be judged by the story it tells , but rather by how
it chooses to tell that particular story. CS Karthikeyan, in his debut feature, tells that story in an outwardly
familiar format but successfully incorporates interesting laugh-out-loud moments into the mix.
The film would not have had half of its impact if not for some of its quick-witted one-liners.
Even when long stretches do not work, all of a sudden, one of the characters cracks ,
a smart jokethat drags you back into what's going on. It also has to be noted that most of the film’s effective
jokes are told by the protagonist Aravind/Saba’s (Ashok Selvan) friends.
Similar to most films in this genre, the film is as much about Aravind’s friendships
as it is about the women he loves. But on hand, while Saba Nayagan succeeds in
effectively portraying the friendship aspect of the protagonist’s life, Aravind's
love life pales in comparison. ButKarthika Muralidharan as Aravind’s school crush
Esha commands the screen with her arresting presence.
Luckily, the film is self-aware in its approach and doesn’t take itself too seriously by
shifting its tonal gears to drama to prove a point. Saba Nayagan is a comedy, and the film
never forgets that fact and also makes sure that we, the audience, also don’t forget this.
A mother being disappointed by her son or friends sharing an emotional hug at a farewell
or the heartbreaks of Aravind and his friends—none of it is prolonged, and some of these moments
are played for laughs rather than emotional sentiments. Speaking of the cast, Jaiseelan Sivaram, Arun Kumar,
and Sriram Krish are hilarious as the protagonist’s friends. As the protagonist,
Ashok Selvan is effective, as he holds together the film with admirable nonchalance.
All said and done, Saba Nayagan is a film that thrives on relatability and references,
if not anything else. Written by: Roopa Radhakrishnan
